Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a layered pasta dish made with wide, flat noodles, typically alternating with layers of tomato sauce, meat sauce, béchamel sauce, cheese, and seasonings. The dish is then baked in an oven until the pasta is cooked through and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Lasagna is a hearty and satisfying dish that is perfect for a special occasion or a casual family meal. It is also a versatile dish that can be adapted to different dietary needs and preferences. For example, vegetarian lasagnas can be made with layers of vegetables, tofu, or lentils instead of meat.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles: Lasagna noodles are typically made with durum wheat flour and eggs. They are wider and flatter than other types of pasta noodles, such as spaghetti or fettuccine.
  • Tomato sauce: Tomato sauce is the base of most lasagna recipes. It is made with tomatoes, onions, garlic, herbs, and spices.
  • Meat sauce: Meat sauce is made with ground beef, sausage, or other meats, cooked in a tomato sauce.
  • Béchamel sauce: Béchamel sauce is a white sauce made with milk, butter, flour, and nutmeg.
  • Cheese: Lasagna is typically topped with a layer of grated cheese, such as mozzarella, Parmesan, or ricotta.

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius).
  2. Make the meat sauce:
    • In a large skillet, brown ground beef or sausage over medium heat. Drain off any excess grease.
    • Add chopped onions and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    • Add a jar of your favorite tomato sauce, a can of diced tomatoes, and your favorite Italian seasonings to the skillet. Bring to a simmer and cook for at least 30 minutes, or up to an hour.
  3. Make the béchamel sauce (optional):
    • In a medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
    • Whisk in flour until smooth.
    • Gradually whisk in milk until the sauce is thickened.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Cook the lasagna noodles:
    • Fill a large pot with salted water and bring to a boil.
    • Add the lasagna noodles to the boiling water and cook according to package directions.
    • Drain the noodles and rinse with cold water.
  5. Assemble the lasagna:
    • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
    • Top with a layer of lasagna noodles.
    • Spread a layer of ricotta cheese over the noodles.
    • Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of noodles and meat sauce.
    • Top with grated mozzarella cheese.
  6. Bake the lasagna for 30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving.
